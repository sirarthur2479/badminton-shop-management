Logging In

The staff panel is at /#/staff — add that to your phone's home screen for quick access from court.

1

Open the URL

Navigate to the staff panel on any browser. You'll see a keypad — this is the PIN gate.

2

Enter the PIN

Type your 4-digit staff PIN. For demo setups, the default PIN is 1234. If you've changed it, use the value set in your environment configuration.

3

You're in

The staff dashboard appears. This session stays unlocked until you tap Logout or close the browser.

Add to home screen In Safari (iOS) or Chrome (Android), use "Add to Home Screen" to pin the staff panel as an app icon. It opens directly to the PIN gate — no URL to remember.

Dashboard Overview

After logging in, you see the staff dashboard — a tile grid with quick access to each section:

TileWhat it does
Order QueueAll stringing orders — tap to manage status and view details
InventoryRacket brands/models and string brands/models — the catalog for the kiosk
Shop ProductsProducts displayed on the public /#/shop page — photos, prices, sale tags
SettingsShop name, tagline, contact info, about blurb, and accent colour
InquiriesCustomer inquiries from the shop page — shows a badge when new ones arrive

If there are unread inquiries, a red badge appears on the Inquiries tile showing the count. It disappears once all inquiries are marked Replied or Closed.

Order Queue

The order queue is the primary view for managing stringing jobs. Orders appear immediately after a customer submits from the kiosk.

Reading the queue

Each row shows: date received, customer name, racket, string + tension, and the current status badge. Rows are sorted newest-first.

Status cycle

Tap any status badge to advance to the next stage:

StatusMeaningTriggers email?
PendingOrder received, not started yet—
In ProgressCurrently being strung—
DoneRestringing complete, ready for pickupYes — emails customer if they provided address
Picked UpCustomer has collected the racket—

Expanding an order

Click anywhere on a row (except the status badge) to expand it. The expanded view shows the full racket and string details, tension, customer name, phone, email, notes, and the submission timestamp.

Managing from your phone The queue works on any screen size. If you're on court and a customer calls about their racket — open the staff URL on your phone, find the order by name, and update the status right there.

Inquiries

When a customer sends an inquiry from the /#/shop page, it appears in the Inquiries panel. The dashboard tile shows a badge with the count of unread inquiries.

Inquiry table

Each row shows: date, customer name, contact (phone or email), number of items in the inquiry, and the status badge:

StatusMeaning
NewUnread — needs a response. Row is bold with a yellow left border.
RepliedYou've contacted the customer
ClosedNo further action needed

Handling an inquiry

1

Click to expand

Tap any row to see the full inquiry — item list with quantities and prices, the customer's message, and their full contact details.

2

Reply to the customer

Use the phone number or email shown in the expanded row to contact the customer directly — outside the app. There's no built-in reply function.

3

Update the status

Tap Mark Replied after you've contacted them, or Mark Closed if no further action is needed. The badge count on the dashboard tile decreases.

Shop Products

Shop products are the items shown on the public /#/shop page. You manage them from the Inventory section of the staff panel.

Adding a product

1

Open Inventory → Shop Products tab

From the dashboard, tap Inventory, then select the Shop Products tab at the top.

2

Fill in the product details

Name, category (Racket / String / Grip / Shuttle / Bag / Shoe), price, and stock quantity. Optional: sale price and sale end date.

3

Upload a photo

Click the image area to upload a product photo (JPG, PNG, or WebP — max 5MB). Or paste an image URL directly. Photos appear in the product grid on the shop page.

4

Or scan a barcode

Tap the barcode icon to use your camera to scan a product's UPC barcode. The app looks up the product name and image automatically. Useful for adding common items quickly.

5

Set visibility

The visible toggle controls whether the product appears on the public shop page. Turn it off to hide a product without deleting it — useful for out-of-season items or items you're not ready to list yet.

Bulk import via CSV

To add many products at once, use the Import CSV button. Download the template first to see the required column format, fill it in, and upload. The import skips rows with missing required fields and shows a summary of what was imported vs skipped.

Sale pricing

On any product's edit form, set a Sale Price and a Sale Ends At date. The sale badge and crossed-out original price appear automatically on the shop page while the sale is active. After the end date, the product reverts to its standard price.

Accent colour Five presets are available. The colour flows through to the shop page's primary buttons and status badges — pick one that matches your shop's visual identity.

String & Racket Catalog

The kiosk stringing form uses this catalog for its dropdowns — racket brands, racket models, string brands, and string models. If a customer's racket or preferred string isn't listed, it won't appear in the kiosk form.

The four tabs

TabWhat it manages
Racket BrandsTop-level brands (Yonex, Victor, Li-Ning, etc.)
Racket ModelsSpecific models per brand (Astrox 99, Nanoflare 1000, etc.) with stock qty and price
String BrandsString manufacturers (Yonex, Victor, Ashaway, etc.)
String ModelsSpecific strings with recommended tension range (min/max lbs), stock qty, and price

Adding an item

Each tab has an "Add" form at the bottom. Fill in the required fields and save. The new item appears in the kiosk dropdowns immediately — no restart required.

Editing and deleting

Each row has edit and delete buttons. Editing a string model lets you update the tension range — the kiosk enforces these limits when customers enter tension. Deleting a brand removes all its models too.

Deleting is permanent Deleted brands and models are gone from the system. Existing orders that referenced them still show the brand and model name (stored as text) — so historical orders are unaffected.
