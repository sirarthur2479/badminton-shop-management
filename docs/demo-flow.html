Demo Script

The 12-Minute Walkthrough

A guided script for showing Badminton Pro Shop to a potential shop owner. Step-by-step, timed, with the right questions at the end.

Pre-demo Checklist

Set this up 5 minutes before the owner walks in. The demo should be live and waiting — not loading while they watch.

  • ✓iPad open at /#/ — stringing kiosk visible, no loading spinner
  • ✓Your phone at /#/staff — logged into staff panel (PIN: 1234)
  • ✓Laptop or second tab at /#/shop — product grid loaded
  • ✓Submit one test order ahead of time so the queue isn't empty — then delete it if you want a clean slate
  • ✓Have a real racket nearby if possible — physical props make it land
Wifi The app needs Supabase access. Check that the venue wifi works — or have a mobile hotspot ready. The kiosk will silently stall if it can't reach Supabase.

1 — Set the Scene 2 min

Don't open the app yet. Start with the problem they already know.

1

Tell the paper-note story

"You're out on court coaching. A customer drops in a racket and leaves a handwritten note. End of the day — you've got five rackets and four notes and you're not sure which is which."

2

One sentence, then hand over the iPad

"This replaces the note. The customer registers their own order. Let me show you — pretend you're the customer."

2 — Customer Kiosk 3 min

Hand the iPad to the owner. Let them drive — don't click it for them.

1

Select racket brand and model

Tap the brand dropdown → pick Yonex. Model → Astrox 99. Watch the model list filter automatically — point this out: "No typos, no guessing."

2

Select string and tension

String brand → Yonex. Model → BG80. Tension slider or field — pick 27 lbs. The tension range enforces the string's rated min/max automatically.

3

Contact details

Enter a name and phone number. Email is optional — mention that if they provide it, they'll get an email when the racket is done.

4

Submit and show the confirmation

Tap Submit. Show the order ID on the confirmation screen. "That's what the customer quotes when they come to pick it up."

Talking point "The customer does this themselves on the kiosk while you're on court. By the time you come in, the order is already in the system with everything structured — no handwriting to decipher."

3 — Staff Order Queue 3 min

Pick up your phone. This is the moment that closes the loop.

1

Show the order they just placed

Open /#/staff on your phone → Inquiries tile. The order from the kiosk appears at the top of the queue. "This is what you'd see from your phone while you're still coaching."

2

Click to expand a row

Tap any row in the order table. It expands inline — full racket, string, tension, and customer contact. No separate screen to navigate to.

3

Cycle the status

Tap the status badge → Pending → In Progress → Done → Picked Up. One tap per transition. "When you mark it Done, the customer gets an email automatically — if they gave their address."

Talking point "You can manage the whole queue from your phone. You don't need to be at the counter to know what's waiting."

4 — Shop Storefront 3 min

Switch to the laptop tab showing /#/shop. This is the public-facing product page.

1

Browse products by category

Show the category tabs — Racket, String, Grip, Shuttle, Bag. Tap Racket. Products load in a grid with photos, prices, and sale badges where applicable.

2

Search for "Yonex"

Type in the search field. Results filter instantly — no page reload. "A customer on their phone can browse your stock before they even come in."

3

Add items to an inquiry

Tap "Add to Inquiry" on 2–3 products. Show the floating badge counting up. "This is like a cart, but instead of buying, they're sending you a price inquiry."

4

Submit the inquiry

Open the inquiry sheet → fill in name + contact → tap Send. Show the success state with inquiry ID.

5 — Staff Inquiries Panel 2 min

Back on the staff panel. Show the inquiry they just submitted.

1

Show the Inquiries tile with the badge

On the staff dashboard, the Inquiries tile has a red badge showing "1 new". "You'd see this the moment you open the staff panel."

2

Expand the inquiry row

Click the row — see the full item list with quantities, the customer's message, and their contact details. "Everything you need to reply — right there."

3

Mark as Replied

Tap "Mark Replied". The badge clears. "The yellow New badge goes away — your team knows someone has handled it."

Closing Questions

Stop showing. Start listening. Ask one at a time — don't rush to the next if they're still talking.

Does this match how your shop actually works today?
What would be missing for this to work in your shop?
Is one PIN for all staff fine, or would you want separate logins?
Would customer text notifications when the racket's ready be useful?
What would make this worth paying for?
What you're listening for Objections about catalog completeness ("we carry brands you don't have"), multi-staff access, and SMS notifications come up most often. All are buildable — note them, don't promise timelines.
